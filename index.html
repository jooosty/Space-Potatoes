Optics System

Wolter Type 1
Mirror Modules

NEBULA-Xplorer focuses X-rays using double-reflecting Wolter type 1 mirror modules — 30 thin-foil gold-coated concentric conical mirrors per concentrator, each just 20 cm long and 10 cm in diameter. Gold coatings maximise X-ray reflectivity at the shallow grazing-incidence angles required.

The compact design results in an 85 cm focal length, directing photons onto individual Silicon Drift Detectors housed in the Focal Plane Assembly. Dedicated alignment modules ensure precise concentration of photons onto each SDD.

Detection System

Silicon Drift Detectors

Each concentrator focuses X-ray photons onto a dedicated Silicon Drift Detector (SDD) — commercial off-the-shelf devices selected for reliability and performance. The flight model carries 12 SDDs in total, with 2 reserved for in-flight calibration using a small radioactive source.

To reach optimal sensitivity, all detectors are cooled to −35°C via thermal strapping to a dedicated radiator on the satellite exterior. Straight-light baffles (black cones) protect each SDD from stray photons. The signal from every SDD is processed by hot-redundant Instrument Readout Units with space-grade digital signal processing.

The IRUs record X-ray energies with precise timing information — enabling the spectral-timing science that defines the NEBULA mission.

Development Roadmap

From Prototype
to Flight

NEBULA-Xplorer follows a two-model philosophy aligned with ECSS standards — minimising risk while building the future space workforce.

Phase 01
Engineering
Model
2025 – 2027
Full engineering model of both instrument and satellite. Verifies design, interfaces, power budgets, launch loads, and instrument performance. Built on existing prototypes and mature off-the-shelf space systems.
Phase 02
Flight
Model
2027 – 2029
Flight-grade hardware built with space-qualified components, incorporating all lessons from the EM phase. Fully tested to ECSS standards. Targeted for SpaceX Falcon 9 launch, adaptable for ESA VEGA-C.
Phase 03
Launch
& Operations
~2030
Satellite launched into orbit for a minimum 5-year (goal 7-year) mission. Continuous X-ray observations of binary black holes and neutron stars. Science data publicly accessible via SRON servers.
Spacecraft Bus

What's Inside

OB
Optical Bench
Houses all 10 concentrators with alignment modules. Aluminium structure with gold-coated thin-foil Wolter mirrors, spider-web support structures.
FPA
Focal Plane Assembly
12 SDDs with light baffles, 2 calibration pixels, and thermal strapping to the external radiator. Cooled to −35°C for optimal performance.
IRU
Instrument Readout Units
Two hot-redundant IRUs with space-grade electronics. Records photon energy and precise timing for all 10 science SDDs simultaneously.
SC
Spacecraft Structure
Aluminium skeleton reinforced with carbon fibre sandwich panels. 100 × 50 × 90 cm for Falcon 9 (expandable for VEGA-C). Includes deorbit propulsion.
ACS
AOCS & Comms
Attitude, orbit and control system for precise pointing. Solar panels, communication, thermal control, and data storage subsystems.
